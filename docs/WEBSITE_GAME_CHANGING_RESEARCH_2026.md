# Website Transformation Research Brief

Date: 2026-05-11

## Goal

Move Sip Studies from a dense beverage-content website into a guided learning and business-training operating system.

The product should feel:

- clear enough for first-time learners
- fast enough for experienced beverage professionals
- premium enough for paid training buyers
- practical enough for hospitality teams
- accessible enough to use with keyboard, mobile, and varied learning needs

## Source Set

Design and interaction:

- Awwwards State of AI 2025: interactive report patterns, on-scroll animation, navigation, timeline, data visualization, storytelling.
- CSS Design Awards and FWA winner/nominee patterns: productized cards, visual craft, motion systems, interactive labs.
- Contemporary design trend references around tactile texture, editorial type, and warmer anti-generic digital surfaces.

Learning and UX:

- Nielsen Norman Group: progressive disclosure and information scent.
- W3C WCAG 2.2: keyboard access, focus visibility, consistent navigation, target size, dragging alternatives, and accessible authentication.
- CAST UDL: multiple means of engagement, representation, and action/expression.
- Duolingo, Khan Academy, Brilliant, Mimo, and Quizlet patterns: personalized paths, mastery states, active recall, spaced review, short interactive cards.
- Retrieval practice, spacing, interleaving, gamification, and self-determination research surfaced by the research agents.

Beverage education and business:

- WSET: progressive beverage qualifications across wine, beer, sake, and spirits, plus business/custom-course lanes.
- GuildSomm: public content plus member-gated study guides, quizzes, maps, forums, and professional network.
- Napa Valley Wine Academy, Commonwealth Wine School, American Wine School, National Wine School: certification-style course pages with outcomes, format, study hours, included materials, and tasting requirements.
- Wine Folly, A Bar Above, DiVino, Somm.Site: approachable entry offers, diagnostic buying help, lower-cost first wins, and confidence-oriented positioning.
- Typsy, GuildSomm group memberships, private tasting/event providers: B2B training, per-seat/business lanes, and corporate events as revenue bridge.

## Strategic Findings

1. The current strongest opportunity is not another global menu. It is a diagnostic front door.
   Beginners need a recommended path. Experts need a fast route. Buyers need a plan. Team managers need training language.

2. Award-winning interaction should be used to clarify learning, not decorate the page.
   Sip Studies should apply immersive patterns as route previews, timeline journeys, tasting labs, maps, and progress visuals.

3. The market splits across credential, confidence, job-skill, and team-training promises.
   Sip Studies should not collapse those into one generic pitch. Each role should get a different action and proof block.

4. The first paid step should feel lower-risk.
   Competitors use free previews, low-cost mini-courses, one-off workshops, and self-paced access before higher-ticket certification or cohort offers.

5. Learning systems retain better when they show the next best action.
   The site should point users to one immediate step: start a map, take a diagnostic, review weak concepts, open a route, compare plans, or enroll.

6. Sipopedia should become an answer engine.
   Glossary cards should answer learner questions: what it means, where it appears, how to taste/apply it, and what exam/service clue matters.

7. Tasting logistics are part of the product.
   Remote beverage learning should eventually expose tasting kits, shopping lists, BYOB options, and tasting-free alternatives.

8. The B2B lane needs its own information architecture.
   Team buyers need seats, reporting, onboarding, staff curriculum bundles, private cohorts, and manager dashboards.

9. Accessibility is not a compliance afterthought.
   Command menus, pathfinders, orbit cards, modals, and carousels need keyboard paths, visible focus, reduced-motion tolerance, and non-drag alternatives.

10. Sip Studies should lean into tactile educational craft.
    The design system should feel like cellar notes, maps, tasting cards, lab dashboards, and mission control instead of generic SaaS gradients.

## Immediate Product Direction

Add a role-based pathfinder to the lobby:

- New Learner: guided Launch Pad preview and low-friction starter route.
- Certification Prep: Sipopedia plus quiz/academy route and Pro plan comparison.
- Working Pro: service-decision training, recipes, maps, and tasting language.
- Team Lead: cohort/team training and B2B language.

Each role should show:

- one recommended next action
- one secondary route
- expected outcome
- time or commitment cue
- what unlocks with paid access

## Next Implementation Slices

1. Lobby pathfinder with role cards and computed next route.
2. Placement diagnostic with 6-10 questions and saved local recommendation.
3. Sipopedia answer-card redesign.
4. Review queue / "before you forget" spaced-practice surface.
5. Team-training landing section with per-seat/cohort/private workshop framing.
6. Course-page outcome blocks: who it is for, what users can do, format, time, includes, tasting requirements, certificate, next step.
7. Interactive lab moments: aroma wheel, label reader, region challenge, pairing simulator.
8. Accessibility pass: focus order, target sizes, reduced motion, and keyboard parity for all new surfaces.

## Design Guardrails

- Keep the command menu compact.
- Use motion only when it improves comprehension or progress.
- Favor visible route scent over mystery navigation.
- Do not add global menu categories without removing or consolidating another surface.
- Every new section must answer: "What should this user do next?"
